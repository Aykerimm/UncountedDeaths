This data is between March and December 2020, and between January and August 2021

What is excess mortality? Based on the past 10 years of data, our team defined a number of expected deaths in each county for 2020 and 2021, if the Covid-19 pandemic never happened. Excess mortality is the difference between these expectations and the number of deaths observed.

What is the proportion unassigned to Covid? When any individual dies, the reason is recorded on their death certificate. We would expect that most of the excess deaths are due to the pandemic, and so are recorded on the death certificate as a Covid death. This metric defines our the excess deaths, how many are officially recorded as being from Covid.

What is the type of medical examiner? Different counties have different ways of investigating the cause of deaths. It is this person's job to ensure that death certificates are accurate. Our team is investigating if counties with different types of medical examiner might have different rates of deaths assigned to Covid-19.
